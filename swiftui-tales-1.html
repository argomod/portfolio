Line height

The journey to translate typographic line height between web, iOS, and Figma highlights my role nicely. I personally think line height is a special detail that makes visual design shine (designer alert!). I really wanted to get it right across all of the platforms.

Our design system uses JSON to store a single source of truth for basic decision decisions across web, iOS, and Figma. That means ideally one value can either be directly injected into our code base or easily translated to work with a platform’s preferred convention. I needed to decide what value makes most sense to store line height in our JSON.

In web, line height is expressed in any unit accepted in CSS; unitless numbers are most accessible. In Figma, line height is expressed in either an integer or percentage.

One consequence I didn’t realize until later is that it’s impossible to provide a line height less than 100% of the font size; that isn’t common in practice, but one of our condensed fonts was affected. I had to use FontForge to adjust the metrics manually.

But an even bigger problem emerged: how SwiftUI distributes line height.

While web and Figma evenly distributes space above and below a line of text, Apple places space only in between lines of text.

[image]

Yes,this is why our designs in Figma always have spacing issues around text! Joe and I call this the “half-leading problem”, to which we have not attempted a programmatic fix but alert our customets to.

Summary

I and the TPL team learned how to produce color, font size, font weight, and spacing in SwiftUI and then used our combined platform knowledge to distribute 100% of our design tokens across all supported platforms (which by our definition now includes Figma). In total this took 1.5 months.
