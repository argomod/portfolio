More about me

I like working with passionate folks & companies whose purpose extends beyond what they sell.

I specialize in designing digital products for diverse users, inclusive of all abilities and backgrounds. My process involves posing questions to users and stakeholders, gathering data, collaborating, pitching, iterating, prototyping, and coding.

I believe good design respects the needs of all humans and the environment.

I also have fun practicing landscape architecture & Japanese carpentry, skateboarding, walking my foster dogs, and reading about design history.

I am currently a product designer at The New York Times. I've also designed for Airbnb, Arcadia, Robinhood, and Hackster.
